About Me

I'm Brandon Khek, a third-year astrophysics student at Rice University. I an undergraduate student interested in early and late universe cosmology, astroparticle physics, and compact objects. Besides my experience in physics, I am also well versed in upper-level college mathematics such as complex analysis and partial differential equations. My extensive experience with computers and programming such as Python, Mathematica, and Unix make me a versatile worker as I prepare for a career in theoretical astrophysics research.

Research

Gravitational waves resulting from Higgs field inhomogeneities

Gravitational waves serve as a window into the very early universe. In this project, I explore what we can learn from primordial gravitational radiation and how it manifests itself today.

  • Early universe cosmology
  • Astroparticle physics
  • MATLAB and Mathematica

Spherical Fourier-Bessel basis power spectrum

Upcoming deep, wide-angle galaxy surveys such as SPHEREx and JWST give us a lot of data to process. In this summer, fellowship, I maximize the extraction of cosmological information from these surveys by creating an SFB power spectrum of density fluctuations in the universe.

  • Large-scale structure
  • Late universe cosmology
  • Julia programming
Visit Project Page →

Axion-photon conversions in neutron star magnetospheres

Axions, a candidate for what comprises cold dark matter, are rather difficult to detect experimentally. There is a possibility of the axions undergoing conversion into detectable radio photons, and here I calculate the probability of this happening.

  • Astroparticle physics
  • Compact objects
  • Mathematica

Teaching

COLL 106: Dissecting Physics Pop Science

COLL is a class code, short for COLLege course, which are student-taught courses. After a semester of training, Rice students can teach a COLL course on anything they'd like. In my course, I aim to give deeper insight on physics popular science topics, such as the Higgs boson and black holes, by exploring the mathematical formulation behind them.

RCEL ELITE Tech Camp

Over the summer of 2020 I had the pleasure of teaching 28 high school students machine learning in Python through RCEL - the Rice Center for Engineering Leadership. We used anomaly detection algorithms to analyze meteorological data. Examples include the Isolation Forest and Local Outlier Factor algorithms, aided with powerful libraries like SciKitLearn and MatPlotLib.
