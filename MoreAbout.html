Background

Origins

I was born in Alexandria, Virginia and have lived in Northern Virginia for my entire life before coming to university. I am cliché in the sense that, like many people, I'll tell you, "I've always been interested in physics since I was a kid..." and all of the other fluff you might not include in a Statement of Purpose, but instead perhaps on the personal page of your website portfolio. There was never really an instrumental catalyst that spontaneously spurred interest in particle physics or astronomy. I've generally just accumulated interest over time. I learned about quarks sometime in 2nd grade, naively asked for an electron microscope for my birthday in 3rd or 4th, and have always done my science and math homework first when I got home from school in 5th and 6th and beyond. There are other extraneous examples, but the point is, this interest has persisted for my entire life and, after convincing myself in my freshman year of college that switching from CS to astrophysics was truly what I wanted to do (and convincing my family that it would not render me jobless upon graduation), I committed to a career in research in hopes of becoming like the scientists I admired.

Heritage

My Cambodian parents immigrated to the United States during the Khmer Rouge to escape the Cambodian genocide. It is an understatement to say that the war had an impact on my family. As an example, my maternal grandfather is the only surviving member of his family. Remarkably, my mother and father put themselves through college and got jobs to support their family and eventually propelled themselves to provide me with food on the table and a roof to live under. I'll be the first in my family to get my Ph.D., and shockingly, only 7% of Cambodian-Americans to do so, compared to 24% of all Asian Americans and 13% of all Americans.

Hobbies and Other Interests

During my sophomore year of high school, I found I like to play chess! Like many others, I find chess to be a brutal game: you can't rely on luck. The absence of all variables except your own skills puts a lot of exciting pressure to perform. Here's a less embarassing game of mine from January 17, 2022. Racquet Sports and Skiing

Tennis is a go-to for exercise, either against the wall or with a friend. I've played since my sophomore year of high school. I have also gotten into Squash in the past year or two, and became pretty good at table tennis since entering college. I engage less frequently in skiing, though have been doing so since I was in third or fourth grade. I try to go over winter breaks when I go back home to Virginia.

Philosophy and Psychology

I really like to talk about philosophy, specifically ethics and metaphysics. What does it mean to live virtuously? Is there a "right thing"? Is there and optimal level of pollution? Is it morally permissible to eat factory farmed meat? What is consciousness? Does the existence of the universe depend on a creator? Do we live in a deterministic world? I could really go on forever about this. I am also intrigued by social psychology. I think it's intesting to look at the way people interact. What makes "popular" people popular? How do social environments affect decision making? How do others affect our perception of ourselves? A great example of the power of social psychology are the Asch conformity experiments.
