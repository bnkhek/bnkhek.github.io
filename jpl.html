Spherical Fourier Bessel Analysis of Large Scale Structure

Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Supervised by Dr. Henry Gebhardt and Dr. Olivier Doré

Summer 2021 - Fall 2022

Project Overview

One of the best statistical estimators of the underlying matter overdensity field is the power spectrum. Measurements of the power spectrum statistic in a Cartesian Fourier basis are suboptimal for analyses of data from upcoming deep (high redshift) wide-angle (large survey area) galaxy redshift surveys such as the High-Latitude Spectroscopic Survey of the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope and SPHEREx. In this project, I developed code to construct the power spectrum in a spherical Fourier Bessel (SFB) basis. This has a variety of advantages compared to the Cartesian basis, such as excluding the need for wide-angle corrections and allowing for easier joint analyses with other cosmological probes such as the CMB. My code provides robust estimates of the SFB power spectrum and is tunable to different survey geometries and cosmologies. I also leverage the program to predict the tightness of galaxy survey constraints on cosmological parameters through Fisher matrix forecasting.

Results

I derived expressions to calculate the SFB power spectrum, which becomes discretized when we impose boundary conditions as dictated by the finite galaxy survey volume. Programming these results, I explored how various physics arises in the SFB power spectrum. One example are redshift-space distortions, which arise due to the redshift/blueshift from the line-of-sight component of galaxies' peculiar velocities, thereby increasing the observed clustering amplitude. Using the Fisher matrix formalism, I also derived cosmic-growth constraints on parameters fNL, Ωm, h, fσ8, and bσ8, shown to the left.
