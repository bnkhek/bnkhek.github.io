The Matter Power Spectrum in a Spherical Fourier Bessel Basis (WIP)

Jet Propulsion Laboratory

Supervised by Dr. Henry Gebhardt and Dr. Olivier Doré

Summer 2021 - Present

Project Overview

One of the best statistical estimators of the underlying matter overdensity field is the power spectrum. Previous measurements of the power spectrum statistic in a Cartesian Fourier basis are suboptimal for analyses of data from upcoming deep (high redshift) wide-angle (large survey area) galaxy redshift surveys such as the High-Latitude Spectroscopic Survey of the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope and SPHEREx. In this project, I developed code to construct the power spectrum in a spherical Fourier Bessel (SFB) basis. This has a variety of advantages compared to the Cartesian basis, such as excluding the need for wide-angle corrections and allowing for easier joint analyses with other cosmological probes such as the CMB. My code provides robust estimates of the SFB power spectrum and is tunable to different survey geometries and cosmologies. I also leverage the program to predict the tightness of upcoming galaxy survey constraints on cosmological parameters through Fisher matrix forecasting.
