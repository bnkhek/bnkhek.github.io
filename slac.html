Gamma Ray Source Localization with Machine Learning

SLAC National Accelerator Laboratory | Supervised by Drs. Aashwin Mishra, Micah Buuck, and Thomas Shutt

Summer 2022 - Fall 2022

Project Overview

GammaTPC is an instrument concept that aims to measure the low MeV sky, which is currently poorly constrained. GammaTPC utilizes the same Time Projection Chamber technology seen in experiments from the XENON collaboration or ALICE at CERN. Incident gamma photons scatter electrons within the vat of liquid argon contained within the observatory, and to most accurately determine where the photon came from, we need to know the kinematic parameters of the scattering event: the location of the event and initial direction of the recoiled electron. My goal in this project is to accurately back out these parameters with a machine learning model to minimize the diffusion of the gamma ray source in the image.

Results

I trained models on a set of approximately 100,000 electron tracks each at a fixed energy. These were very performant in that we achieved sub-mm accuracy for the interaction location predictions and initial scattering direction errors usually on the order of a few degrees. The models were not as accurate when trained on a broader data set consisting of varying energies, showcasing a limitation of the work which may be resolved with a more complex archiecture optimized for the larger data set.

Direction error for the initial direction model trained on one energy, drift distance, and pixel pitch. The model is highly accurate for small drift distances, though decreases in performance as the distance increases due to charge diffusion.
