Axion-Photon Conversion Rates in Neutron Star Magnetospheres

Rice University

Supervised by Dr. Andrew Long, Dr. Mustafa Amin (2021)

Spring 2021, Fall 2022 - Present

Project Overview

The weak coupling of the hypothetical axion, a candidate for what comprises dark matter, to photons motivates observational efforts to find electromagnetic signatures of axions. Strong magnetic fields are conducive for the mixing of axions into photons and vice versa. Such extreme magnetic fields are found in natural astrophysical laboratories such as neutron stars, which I aim to study in this project. I leverage the axion-photon coupling and perform analytic calculations for the probability of the resonant conversion of axions into photons. Then, I numerically find solutions for the probability in various ambient environmental conditions such as different magnetic field and plasma density profiles.
